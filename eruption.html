ERUPTION

Volcanic eruption is the expulsion of gases, rock fragments, and/or molten lava from within the Earth through a vent onto the Earth's surface or into the atmosphere. Volcanic eruptions have the potential to destroy a lot of lives and property. They might be mild, as those frequently seen in Hawaiian volcanoes, or extremely destructive, like the Vesuvius eruption that obliterated Pompeii in 79 CE.

Frequency from 2000 - 2023
Impact to Communities

Health concerns after a volcanic eruption include infectious disease, respiratory illness, burns, injuries from falls, and many more

Many have fallen victim to falling rocks from a volcanic blast.

Habitats and landscapes are damaged by lava flows.

Ash ejected by the volcano acts as a good fertiliser for soils.

Rising magma brings valuable minerals to the surface, creating mining opportunities.

The high level of heat and activity inside the earth, close to a volcano, can provide opportunities for generating geothermal energy
