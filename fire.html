WILDFIRE

Wildfire is an uncontrolled and rapidly spreading fire that happens in vegetation and natural settings. It is frequently fueled by dry weather, high temperatures, and strong winds. Wildfires can inflict enormous damage to forests, wildlife habitats, and human infrastructure. They can also endanger human safety and health due to the production of smoke and air pollution. Firefighters and emergency response teams work to control and extinguish wildfires, while prevention methods include managing vegetation, enforcing fire safety legislation, and promoting public knowledge about fire safety.

Frequency from 2000 - 2023
Impact to Communities

Wildfire ash becomes an important source of nutrients for the soil. Ash sediment contains calcium, magnesium, potassium and phosphorous

Air pollution can cause a range of health issues, including respiratory and cardiovascular problems.

Another significant health effect of wildfires is on mental health and psychosocial well-being.

Wildfire help plant species to reproduce and grow. There are many plant species that depend on fire to reproduce

Wildfire lead to economic losses. Example are decline in tourism sick people’s bills and pollution.

People who are caught unaware often lose their lives during the wildfire events. The smoke, heat, and flames can also kill them.

Huge and intense fire can kill even the fastest animal and lose their habitat.

Wildfire lead to erosion. Wildfires affect the property of soils. As a result, water cannot infiltrate the ground during rainfall.

Erosion can lead to secondary hazards like flooding and landslide immediately after the fire event.
