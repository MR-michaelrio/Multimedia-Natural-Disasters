EARTHQUAKE

Earthquake is a natural occurrence caused by a sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust, which causes the ground to tremble. It is usually generated by the movement and interaction of tectonic plates, which are enormous chunks of the Earth's lithosphere floating on the semi-fluid asthenosphere below. When tension builds up at plate boundaries, it can finally overcome the friction that holds the plates together and cause an earthquake.

Earthquakes can range in size from little vibrations that go unnoticed to massive occurrences that cause significant damage and loss of lives. The Richter scale or the moment magnitude scale, which quantifies the amount of energy produced, is frequently used to assess the intensity of an earthquake.

Frequency from 2000 - 2023 (Magnitude 5+)
Impact to Communities

Earthquakes help the earth to release its stored up energy in them. This energy release helps to keep the earth in good shape.

Landslides triggered by earthquakes cause formation of lakes as happened at many places in the Himalayan region.

Make mineral resources available for economical extraction. Mineral deposits commonly form underground.

Tsunami and landslide may occur due to sustained vibrations and faulting.

Earthquakes can lead to loss of life and injuries due to collapsing buildings, falling debris, and other hazards.

Earthquakes can cause severe damage to infrastructure, including buildings, roads, bridges, and utilities.

Earthquakes can disrupt essential services such as electricity, water supply, transportation, and communication networks.

The cost of rebuilding infrastructure, repairing damages, and providing emergency services can be substantial.
